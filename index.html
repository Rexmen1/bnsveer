Introduction

What is Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023?

The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 is India's new criminal code that came into effect on July 1, 2024, replacing the colonial-era Indian Penal Code (IPC) of 1860.

Historical Context

From Colonial Legacy to Modern Justice

The Indian Penal Code (1860)

  • Drafted by Thomas Babington Macaulay
  • Chairman of the first Law Commission
  • Based on colonial-era legal framework
  • Served India for 164 years

Why Change Was Needed

  • Outdated Provisions: Many sections were no longer relevant
  • Colonial Mindset: Punishment-oriented rather than justice-oriented
  • Technological Gap: Inadequate coverage of cyber crimes
  • Social Evolution: Society has undergone multi-dimensional changes
  • Gender Recognition: Limited recognition of transgender rights

  • Criticized for vague language

‚úÖ What's Added: Acts Endangering Sovereignty

  • Excite or attempt to excite:
    • Secession
    • Armed rebellion
    • Subversive activities
  • Encourage feelings of:
    • Separatist activities
  • Endanger:
    • Sovereignty of India
    • Unity and integrity of India

‚ö†Ô∏è Criticism

Section 377 Removal

Eliminating Discriminatory Provisions

‚ùå What Was Removed

Section 377 of IPC - "Unnatural offenses" provision that criminalized:

  • Consensual same-sex relations (already struck down by Supreme Court)
  • Non-consensual same-sex acts
  • Bestiality

‚öñÔ∏è Supreme Court Context

Section 377 was partially read down in Navtej Singh Johar vs. UOI (2018):

  • Decriminalized: Consensual same-sex relations between adults
  • Retained: Non-consensual acts and bestiality

üö® Legal Gap Created

Complete removal of Section 377 means:

  • No provision for rape of men
  • No provision for bestiality
  • Legal protection gap for male victims

ü§î Criticism

Legal experts argue that BNS should have:

  • Retained non-consensual provisions
  • Created gender-neutral rape laws
  • Maintained bestiality as offense
